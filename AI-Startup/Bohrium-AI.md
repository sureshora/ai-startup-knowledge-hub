1️⃣ What Bohrium Actually Is

Bohrium is an AI-powered research platform for scientists and researchers. It acts like an AI academic search engine + research assistant + research workspace combined in one system.

Instead of searching the web like ChatGPT or Google, it searches scientific literature, journals, and patents and then uses AI to explain them.

Key idea:
➡️ “AI for Science” platform that accelerates scientific research.

The platform integrates huge datasets including:

170M+ research papers

160M+ patents

20M+ scholar profiles across many disciplines.

🔎 Core Features
1. AI Scientific Search

You can ask research questions like:

“How does reinforcement learning improve protein folding?”

“Latest research on quantum batteries”

It returns:

research papers

summarized findings

citations

This works like Google Scholar + ChatGPT combined.

2. “Paper Chat” (Talk to Research Papers)

One of the coolest features:

Upload or open a paper → ask questions like:

explain methodology

summarize results

compare with other papers

The AI analyzes the paper and answers.

3. Research Database

Bohrium includes:

journals

patents

academic datasets

scholar profiles

It covers 26 academic disciplines and 140K+ journals.

4. Research Workspace

Researchers can also:

save papers to a knowledge base

organize research libraries

collaborate with others

run scientific computing tasks.

5. AI Research Assistant

It can help with:

literature review

citation generation

research idea exploration

paper summaries.

👍 Strengths

1. Massive scientific database
Huge academic data coverage.

2. AI explanations for complex papers
Saves hours of reading.

3. Cross-disciplinary research discovery
Finds connections between fields.

4. Designed specifically for scientists
Not generic AI.

👎 Weaknesses

1. Not useful for general users
It is designed mainly for researchers, PhD students, and scientists.

2. Daily limits in free plan
Some searches may be limited.

3. New platform
Community and ecosystem still smaller than tools like Google Scholar.
